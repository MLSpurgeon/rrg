Information about this Feature

The landform I chose to focus on is Grays Arch. It is a sloping arch in the Daniel Boone National Forest. As you can see from the photo on the right it starts from a higher terrain and the arch concludes down a sloping terrain. At the gaps, the tallest section is around 80 feet high of the ground below it. You could fit a bus at the base of it, that’s how wide the width of the widest section is.

To get to Grays Arch we took the Grays Arch Trail, I have included the official trail map below. The first segment is smooth and then the trail gets rougher and downsloping. You can catch glimpses of the arch through the openings in the trees during the last portion of the trail. It’s just a taste of the large scale of the Arch. At that point, you will reach staircases leading down to the low valley adjacent to the arch. After the twists and turns in the last portion of the trail, you can do a bit of bouldering to stand right under the gap of the Arch. Looking up to see the layers of rock and speckled stone at the wall of the rock the arch protrudes out of is beautiful. I was lucky enough to see the arch on a sunny day in November, the trees and brush around were coated with different fall colors.
